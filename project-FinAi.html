Project Overview

FinAI – Investor & Startup Intelligence Platform

FinAI is an AI-powered investment matching and advisory platform that intelligently connects startups, investors, and financial products using large language models (LLMs), vector search, and graph analytics. It simplifies fundraising and deal sourcing by providing explainable, data-driven recommendations rather than black-box outputs. The platform ingests pitch decks, investor theses, and product catalogs, transforms them into semantic embeddings, and enables precise, constraint-aware matchmaking based on goals, stage, geography, and check size. FinAI’s recommendation engine combines retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), cross-encoder reranking, and strict JSON validation for consistent and transparent results. Founders can upload materials to discover thesis-fit investors with clear “why” explanations, while investors can surface startups aligned with their sector and stage focus. A CrewAI-powered advisory mode orchestrates multiple AI agents for in-depth research, portfolio insights, and partnership strategies. Built with React, Tailwind, FastAPI, Weaviate, and Neo4j, FinAI delivers a polished, responsive experience with interactive dashboards, filters, and visual relationship graphs. By unifying intelligence, explainability, and automation, FinAI empowers founders and investors to make faster, smarter, and more confident funding decisions.

Importance of Project

  • Why it matters: Fundraising is noisy and slow; founders struggle to find true thesis-fit investors and warm paths.
  • Who it helps: Founders, investors, and platform/accelerator teams running deal flow.
  • Current struggle: Manual research across scattered docs, cold outreach, and generic shortlists.
  • Alternatives: Crunchbase, PitchBook, Affinity—great data, but limited AI-native, end-to-end matching and explainable rankings.

Research & Insights

Interviews with founders and platform teams revealed the need for explainable matching (not a black box), reliable constraint handling (stage/geo/check size), and reusable research context. We benchmarked retrieval and reranking (bi-encoder + cross-encoder) and validated strict JSON LLM outputs for stable UI rendering.

Ideation and Process

We evolved from simple “profile → product” matching to a richer tri-entity design: Startups ↔ Investors ↔ Financial Products. We added hard-constraint filtering (code-enforced), cross-encoder reranking, and optional graph signals (co-investor proximity, sector overlap). The Crew flow chains RAG research with the recommender, producing an advisor-style summary and next steps.

Architecture & Core Flows

  • Layer 1 – Ingestion: /ingest/startups, /ingest/investors, /ingest/products, /profiles store canonical text + embeddings in Weaviate.
  • Layer 2 – Intelligence: /insights (RAG Q&A), /match/investors and /match/startups (candidate gen → constraints → cross-encoder → LLM JSON), /crew/advice (multi-agent).
  • Layer 3 – Graph (optional): /graph/sync, /graph/query to build/query relationships in Neo4j.

Active Endpoints:

  1. PDF/RAG: POST /ingest, POST /insights
  2. Catalog: POST /ingest/products
  3. Profiles: POST /profiles
  4. Startup & Investor Ingestion: POST /ingest/startups, POST /ingest/investors
  5. Matching: POST /match/investors, POST /match/startups
  6. Crew: POST /crew/advice
  7. Graph: POST /graph/sync, POST /graph/query (optional)

Design Execution

  • Tools & Stack: React (frontend), Tailwind + shadcn/ui, FastAPI/Express (API), Weaviate (vector DB), Neo4j (graph), Groq/OpenAI (LLMs), Crew orchestration, Postgres (ops metadata).
  • Key Components: Match cards with “why” tooltips, strict-JSON explanation panel, filter chips (stage/geo/check), insights panel with citations, graph mini-map.
  • Reliability: Temperature 0, schema validation for LLM JSON, hard constraints enforced in code, diversity by firm/geo.

My Learnings:

  • Precision beats recall: Cross-encoder reranking + hard constraints drastically improve top-5 quality.
  • Trust UX: Users value visible constraints and clear “why” explanations over opaque magic.
  • Composable flows: RAG + Recommender + Graph + Crew provides an explainable advisory loop.

Usability Testing & Validation

With 12 pilot founders and 3 platform teams, we iterated on filters, card density, and JSON error handling. Results: higher shortlist acceptance, fewer false matches, and faster outreach decisions. Offline evals used nDCG@10, Precision@5, and constraint-violation rate.

Reflection and Takeaways

Problems Identified: Black-box matches, constraint leakage, duplicate firms in top-N.

What Worked: Two-stage ranking, schema-validated JSON, diversity rules, “why” explanations.

What Didn’t (early): LLM-only ranking → inconsistent outputs; fixed via cross-encoder + validators.

Key Takeaway: Accurate, explainable matches come from combining retrieval, constraints, reranking, and human-readable reasons.
